What does "short gamma" actually mean for my trade?
◆ CONFLUENCE
Dealers sold options to retail and funds. To stay neutral, they hedge by buying stock when price rises and selling when it falls. When they're short gamma, those hedges amplify the move — they're forced to chase.

For your NVDA long vol trade: if price breaks 145, dealer buying adds fuel — the move extends instead of fading. That's why the setup is better than the same break on a stock where dealers are long gamma (they'd be selling into it, pinning the move).
